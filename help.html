How to Play Checkers

Checkers is a classic board game played between two players on an 8x8 board. Here are the basic rules:

  1. The board consists of 64 squares in alternating dark and light colors.
  2. Each player starts with 12 pieces placed on the dark squares of three rows closest to them.
  3. The pieces can move diagonally forward but can only occupy dark squares.
  4. To move, select one of your pieces and move it diagonally to an adjacent unoccupied square.
  5. If your piece reaches the opponent's back row, it gets "kinged," allowing it to move backward as well.
  6. You can capture your opponent's pieces by jumping over them diagonally if the adjacent square is empty.
  7. A player wins by capturing all of their opponent's pieces

Objective

The primary objective is to capture all of your opponent's pieces!

Additional Information

White is always first in the turn order! Player 1 is white, and Player 2 is black.

WHITE KING = ORANGE, BLACK KING = PURPLE

Scoring

+10 for capturing a piece, +20 for making a piece a king, +1000 for winning the game!
